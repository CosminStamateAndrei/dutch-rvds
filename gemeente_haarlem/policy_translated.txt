About Coordinated Vulnerability Disclosure (formerly Responsible Disclosure)
The IBD attaches great importance to the security of its systems and those of the municipalities. Despite all precautionary measures, it remains possible that a weak point may be found in the systems. If you discover a weak point in one of our systems, we would like to hear from you so that we can quickly take appropriate measures. By submitting a report, the IBD will handle your report in accordance with the agreements below.

We ask the following of you:
- Email your findings to [email protected]. If possible, encrypt the findings with our PGP key to prevent the information from falling into the wrong hands.
- Provide sufficient information to reproduce the problem so that we can resolve it as soon as possible. Usually, the IP address or URL of the affected system and a description of the vulnerability are sufficient, but more may be required for more complex vulnerabilities. - We welcome tips that help us resolve the problem. Please limit yourself to verifiable facts relating to the vulnerability you have identified and avoid your advice essentially amounting to advertising for specific (security) products.

- Please leave contact details so that we can get in touch with you to work together towards a secure outcome. Please leave at least one email address or phone number.

- Please submit the report as soon as possible after discovering the vulnerability.

The following actions are not permitted:
- Installing malware, neither on our systems nor on those of others;
- So-called “brute-forcing” access to systems, except insofar as this is strictly necessary to demonstrate that security in this area is seriously deficient, that is to say, if it is extraordinarily easy to crack a password using publicly available and affordable hardware and software, thereby seriously compromising the system; - Using social engineering, except to the extent strictly necessary to demonstrate that employees with access to sensitive data generally (seriously) fail in their duty to handle it with care. That is to say, if it is generally too easy, in an otherwise completely legal manner (i.e., not through blackmail or the like), to persuade them to provide such data to unauthorized persons. In doing so, you must exercise all care that can reasonably be expected of you so as not to harm the employees concerned themselves. Your findings must be aimed exclusively at demonstrating apparent deficiencies in the procedures and working methods within the IBD and not at harming individual persons employed by the IBD;

- Disclosing or providing information about the security problem to third parties before it has been resolved;
- Performing actions that go beyond what is strictly necessary to demonstrate and report the security problem. In particular regarding the processing (including viewing or copying) of confidential data to which you have had access through the vulnerability. Instead of copying an entire database, you can normally suffice with, for example, a directory listing. Modifying or deleting data in the system is never permitted;

- Using techniques that reduce the availability and/or usability of the system or services (DoS attacks);
- Misusing the vulnerability in any (other) way whatsoever.

What we promise
- If you comply with all the above conditions, we will not file a criminal complaint against you nor initiate civil proceedings against you.
- If it turns out that you have violated any of the above conditions, we may still decide to take legal action against you.
- We treat a report confidentially and do not share a reporter's personal data with third parties without their permission, unless we are required to do so by law or a court ruling.
- The IBD ensures that municipalities share their experiences in this area with one another.
- By mutual agreement, if you wish, we can mention your name as the discoverer of the reported vulnerability. In all other cases, you will remain anonymous. - We will send you an (automatic) acknowledgment of receipt within 1 working day and keep you informed of the progress of the solution.
