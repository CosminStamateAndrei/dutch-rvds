General
The Municipality of Breda considers the security of information and communication technology important. Although the municipality pays full attention to this, a weak point may still exist. If you believe you have found a weak point in the municipality's digital services, the municipality would like to cooperate to resolve the vulnerability as quickly as possible.

What the municipality asks of you
- Report findings as soon as possible.
- Provide as much information as possible to reproduce the problem.
- Often, the IP address or the link (URL) of the affected system and a description of the vulnerability are sufficient.
- More may be required for more complex vulnerabilities.
- Leave your details so that the municipality can work with you to find a solution.

- Reporting under a pseudonym is possible.
- Anonymous reporting is also possible, but cooperation and being kept informed will not be possible in that case.
- Do not share the discovery or sensitive data with others until the problem has been resolved.
- Handle knowledge about the security issue responsibly. For example, by:

- Not retrieving more data than is necessary to demonstrate the vulnerability.
- Not viewing, deleting, or modifying data.
- Not sharing access with others.
- Not placing your own ‘back-door’ in the system.
- Deleting all data retrieved via the security issue as soon as possible.
- Not launching attacks on physical security, social engineering, malware deployment, distributed denial of service, spam, third-party applications, etc.

After the report
- Within 5 working days, the municipality will respond to the report with an assessment and the expected resolution date.
- The municipality will not take criminal action as long as the conditions are met.
- The municipality does not share personal data with third parties without permission, unless required by law or by a court ruling.
- You will be kept informed of the progress in resolving the vulnerability.

Publishing resolved vulnerability
The Municipality of Breda would like to be involved in advance in creating a publication regarding the resolved vulnerability.
