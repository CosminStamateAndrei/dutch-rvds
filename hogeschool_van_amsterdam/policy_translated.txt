The security of our systems is a high priority at the HvA. Nevertheless, vulnerabilities can occur. Anyone who discovers a vulnerability can report it. This allows us to take measures as quickly as possible to remedy it and ensure security.

We refer to this as ‘responsible disclosure’ when the reporter and the organization jointly disclose ICT vulnerabilities, based on a policy established by the organization for this purpose. It is also known as ‘Responsible Disclosure’ or ‘Coordinated Vulnerability Disclosure’.

**Weaknesses as a threat**

A vulnerability is a weak point in software or hardware that is usually the result of a programming error. A vulnerability can be exploited by cybercriminals and poses a threat to the security of information systems.

**Report a vulnerability**

If you wish to report a vulnerability, please fill out the form on our partner's page (opens in a new window), Zerocopter.

**Conditions for reporting**

- Do not exploit vulnerabilities, for example by downloading more data than necessary to demonstrate the vulnerability. - Be extra cautious with personal data: that is, do not view, delete, or modify third-party data.

- Do not share vulnerabilities with others until they have been resolved. And delete all confidential data obtained through vulnerabilities as soon as possible.

- Do not use attacks on physical security or third-party applications, social engineering, distributed denial-of-service, or spam.
- Provide sufficient information to reproduce the vulnerability so that we can resolve it quickly. Usually, an IP address or URL of the affected system and a description of the vulnerability are sufficient, but more may be required for more complex vulnerabilities.

What we promise
- We will respond to your report within 5 days with our assessment and an expected date for a solution.
- We will treat your report confidentially and will not share your personal data with third parties without your permission unless it is necessary to comply with a legal obligation.
- We will keep you informed, if you wish, of the progress in resolving the problem.
- We can mention your name as the discoverer in communications regarding the reported problem, if you wish. - It is possible to report anonymously or under a pseudonym. Please note that this does mean we cannot contact you regarding, for example, follow-up steps, progress in patching the vulnerability, publication, or a potential reward for the report.

The HvA offers a reward as thanks for the help. Depending on the severity of the security issue and the quality of the report, this reward can vary from a small token of appreciation to gift vouchers. However, it must concern a serious security issue that is still unknown to the HvA.

- We strive to resolve reported issues as quickly as possible. We would like to be involved in any potential publication about the issue after it has been resolved.
