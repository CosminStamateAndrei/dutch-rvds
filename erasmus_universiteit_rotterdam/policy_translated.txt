At Erasmus University Rotterdam, we work hard to maintain and improve the security of our systems; nevertheless, vulnerabilities may occur within our systems. With this Responsible Disclosure policy, we ask anyone who discovers a vulnerability to inform us before disclosing it to the outside world. This enables us to take timely measures.

Please note: this Responsible Disclosure policy is not an invitation to extensively and actively scan our corporate network to discover vulnerabilities. We continuously monitor our network ourselves. Consequently, there is a high probability that a scan will be detected, that our CERT will conduct an investigation, and that unnecessary costs may be incurred.

How can we ensure secure systems together?

We ask you:

- to send your findings as soon as possible to the email address cert@eur.nl; preferably encrypt your findings using our EUR PGP key or the web form to prevent the information from falling into the wrong hands. Please refer to the web form for this. - not to misuse vulnerabilities, for example by downloading more data than necessary to demonstrate the vulnerability or by viewing, deleting, or modifying third-party data, and to exercise extra caution regarding personal data;

- not to share vulnerabilities with others until they have been resolved and to delete all confidential data obtained through vulnerabilities as soon as possible;

- not to use attacks on physical security or third-party applications, social engineering, brute-force password attacks, distributed denial-of-service, or spam;

- to provide sufficient information to reproduce the vulnerability so that we can resolve it quickly.

- Usually, an IP address or URL of the affected system and a description of the vulnerability are sufficient, but more may be required for more complex vulnerabilities.

What we promise:
- if you have complied with the above requests, we will not take legal action against you regarding the report. The Public Prosecution Service always retains the right to decide whether you will be criminally prosecuted.
- we will respond to your report within three days with our assessment and an expected date for a solution; - We will treat your report confidentially and will not share your personal data with third parties without your consent unless this is necessary to comply with a legal obligation;

- We will keep you informed of the progress in resolving the issue;
- In communications regarding the reported issue, we will, if you wish, mention your name as the discoverer of the vulnerability;
- Reporting anonymously or under a pseudonym is possible. It is important for you to know that this does mean we will not be able to contact you regarding, for example, follow-up steps, progress in patching the vulnerability, publication, or any potential reward for the report;

- As a thank you for your help, we offer a reward for every first report of a vulnerability unknown to us. The size of the reward is determined by the severity of the vulnerability and the quality of the report and varies from an honorable mention to a donation.
- We strive to resolve all vulnerabilities as quickly as possible. What is not considered a vulnerability:

- Intentional directory content listing(s) for research or publication purposes
- SPF, DKIM, DMARC issues
- Missing secure or http only flags
- Notification of outdated software or upgrade opportunity without an associated exploit and proof of concept
- Missing DNSSEC information
- xmlrpc.php accessibility
- Clickjacking
Erasmus University Rotterdam also offers internet access, subdomains, and hosting facilities for students, associations, and incubator start-up companies. Although these sites are located within the university network, they are not under the (full) control of the university. Responsible disclosure reports regarding these sites are accepted and the reports are forwarded to the responsible persons, but are subsequently closed by the university. These reports do not result in a place in the Hall of Fame, and no progress report is provided.
